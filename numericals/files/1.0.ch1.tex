\section{Some Telescoping Trickery}

\exm{$1(1!)+2(2!)+\cdots+n(n!)=(n+1)!-1$ 
}{Simple apsplication of \textbf{telescoping} trick. Write $j(j!)=(j+1-1)j!=(j+1)j!-j!=(j+1)!-j!$ which gives us $s=\sum_{j=1}^{n} (j)(j!)=\sum_{j=1}^n (j+1)!-j!$ which is just $(2!-1!)+(3!-2!)+\cdots +(n!-(n-1)!)+(n+1!-n!)=(n+1)!-1$}
